# Public vs Private Degree Alignment (Exploratory)

## Data and scope
- Similarity pairs analyzed: 35
- Jobs with at least one matched program: 12
- Jobs with both public and private options: 5
- Join losses (similarity row without degree metadata): 0

## Key descriptive findings
- Pair-level mean similarity: public 0.766, private 0.714.
- Public option coverage lower than private, but overlap exists for substantial subset of jobs.
- Job-level frontier reveals where public option exceeds private benchmark and vice versa.

## Policy interpretation cautions
- Sample still small; treat as directional evidence, not final causal claim.
- Results depend on rule-based candidate gating before similarity stage.
- Next step should test robustness under alternative gating thresholds and provider/model choices.
